Neuroplasticity.
To give a bit of background neuroplasticity is the brains ability to rewire itself with some work, it can also be used by people with some kinds of brain damage like people who have had a stroke to re train their brain to do some tasks that they cant do because of it. Now because of how the brain works if you understand how neuroplasticity works and with some effort you can train yourself to do things almost anything. Now neuroplasticity is useful to understand and knowing a little more about it means I can start to understand why I do some things and how it leads me to learn new things, I put things in to context to understand how things fit together and to makes learning things a little easier. some of the ways to increase you neuroplasticity include reparation, if you repeat a task over and over again the brain will create neural path ways that make that task easier, you could also link the raw information to a context or already cemented knowledge in your head, its easier to recall larger chunks of information that small or individual bits of information.

The growth mindset.
Now the growth mindset is the belief that a person can change over time or learn things they are not good at, this is directly opposed be the fixed mind set which is the belief that people are only good at some things and some things are impossible for some people to learn. In learning about both mind sets I discovered something interesting I hold both mindsets depending on how lazy I feel toward the topic, the key thing to remember is you can learn anything it just depends on how much effort you are willing to put in. while going through this I know I can use the growth mindset to my advantage, I know I can learn this it just needs me to put in enough effort to do this but but I know I can do this.
